How Credit Score Works?

Your credit score is a 3-digit number that reflects how responsibly you use credit. It’s calculated based on factors like:

  • Payment History – Do you pay bills on time?

  • Credit Utilization – How much of your credit limit are you using?

  • Credit Age – How long have you had credit accounts?

  • Credit Mix – Do you have different types of credit (cards, loans)?

  • Recent Inquiries – Have you applied for new credit recently?

Lenders use this score to decide if they can trust you with loans or credit cards. The higher the score, the better your chances!

How often does my credit score change?

Your credit score can update as frequently as your credit report does—usually every 30 days, depending on when lenders report your activity.

Will checking my own credit score lower it?

Not at all! Checking your score through our platform is a soft inquiry, which doesn’t affect your credit in any way.

What’s a good credit score?

Scores range from 300 to 850. A score above 700 is considered good, while 750+ is excellent!

Can I improve my credit score quickly?

Yes! Paying bills on time, reducing debt, and avoiding new hard inquiries can boost your score in just a few months.
